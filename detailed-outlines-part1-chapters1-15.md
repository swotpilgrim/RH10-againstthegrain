# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: Against the Grain

## Chapter 1: Wrong Side of Right

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Wade Turner, first person  
**Setting:** Turner Family Farm and Oakdale High School  
**Time:** Early morning through afternoon, late May 1957

**Purpose:** Establish Wade's rebellion, his conflict with his father, and his sense of being trapped by expectations.

**Beats:**
- Wade wakes bruised from drag strip fight, resents farm work ahead, drags through morning chores while Marcus watches with familiar disappointment
- Tense breakfast where Marcus mentions county agriculture fair in August, suggests Wade enter something, Wade responds with silence and leaves without eating
- At school, Wade called to principal's office about the fight, another lecture about wasted potential and disappointing his father, Wade walks out mid-sentence
- Drive home past endless cotton fields that feel like prison bars, Wade reflects on being the town disappointment, the rebel son who won't fall in line

**Dialogue Topics:** Marcus's disappointment about Wade's choices, principal's lecture about potential and family legacy, Wade's internal monologue about feeling trapped, brief exchange with classmate about the fight

**Emotional Arc:** Wade starts defensive and numb to disappointment, builds to simmering resentment throughout the day, ends with resigned anger at being unable to be what everyone wants.

**Key Details:** Bruised knuckles, empty breakfast plate, cotton fields stretching endlessly, worn leather jacket, hot Georgia morning, Marcus's weathered hands, principal's office smelling like disappointment

**Emotional Arc:** Wade starts with resigned tension around his brother's contentment, finds brief peace in mechanical work, crashes when his father ruins the moment, ends with desperate need to escape.

**Key Details:** Older brother's wedding ring catching sunlight, grease under Wade's fingernails, satisfaction of fixing complex machinery, Marcus's almost-smile, empty country roads at night, dashboard glow, Elvis on the radio

## Chapter 8: What We're Running From

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words  
**POV:** Wade Turner, first person  
**Setting:** Overlook after drag races  
**Time:** Late Saturday night, mid-June

**Purpose:** Major vulnerability moment where Wade and Audrey share true reasons for their escapes and find understanding.

**Beats:**
- After races, Audrey asks Wade why he does it - racing, fighting, the reputation, Wade gives practiced answer about being good at it and pissing people off
- Audrey doesn't buy it, just looks at him steadily until he gets uncomfortable with her silence and honesty
- Wade tells truth: mother died when he was twelve, father retreated into farm and expectations, Wade suffocating under becoming someone he's not, racing only time he feels himself
- Audrey shares her truth: mother wanted specific kind of southern belle life, Audrey wanted to work with hands and fix things, mother called it unladylike, father called it practical

**Dialogue Topics:** Real reasons for rebellion versus surface reasons, mother's death and father's withdrawal, expectations of femininity versus authenticity, difference between missing people and missing prescribed lives

**Emotional Arc:** Wade starts defensive with practiced answers, breaks down walls under Audrey's patient questioning, reaches rare vulnerability sharing about his mother, finds unexpected kinship and relief in being understood.

**Key Details:** Night sky over valley, truck tailgate, Wade's practiced deflections crumbling, Audrey's steady gaze, admission about mother's death, shared understanding in silence, relief of being seen
